Sachin Tendulkar

THE GOD OF CRICKET

"I hate losing and cricket being my first love, once I enter the ground it's a different zone altogether and that hunger for winning is always there."

Here's a timeline of Sachin Tendulkar's life:

1973 Born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India on April 24.
1984 Started his cricket journey under coach Ramakant Achrekar.
1987 Served as a ball boy during the 1987 World Cup.
1988 Scored his first century in school cricket, leading to widespread recognition.
1989 Made his debut for the Indian cricket team at the age of 16 against Pakistan.
1990 Scored his first Test century against England at Old Trafford.
1994 Scored his first ODI century against Australia in Colombo.
1996 Became the leading run-scorer in the Cricket World Cup.
1997 Named the captain of the Indian cricket team.
1998 Played a crucial role in India's victory in the Sharjah Cup, famously known as the "Desert Storm."
1999 Became the highest run-scorer in World Cup history during the tournament.
2001 Scored his 10,000th run in ODI cricket.
2005 Broke Sunil Gavaskar's record of 34 Test centuries.
2008 Surpassed Brian Lara's record for the most runs in Test cricket.
2010 Became the first player to score a double century in an ODI, against South Africa.
2011 Played a significant role in India winning the Cricket World Cup.
2012 Announced his retirement from ODIs.
2013 Retired from Test cricket after playing his 200th Test match, which was also his final international appearance.
2014 Became the first sportsperson to receive the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
2019 Inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame.
2020 Continues to inspire as a mentor and philanthropist, contributing to various social causes.


"I have seen God. He bats at No. 4 for India."
-- Former Australian cricketer Matthew Hayden.
